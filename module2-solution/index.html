HCR RNA-FISH

Probes

HCR Probes, Amplifiers and Buffers enable multiplexed, quantitative, high-resolution fluorescence in situ hybridization (RNA-FISH) with automatic background suppression throughout the protocol. An HCR split-initiator HCR Probes comprise of mutiple HCR Probe pairs that hybridize to different subsequences along the target.

Amplifiers

An HCR Amplifier comprises metastable fluorescent HCR hairpins h1 and h2. Specifically bound HCR Probe pairs that localize the full initiator i1 trigger growth of a tethered fluorescent HCR Amplification polymer. All HCR Amplifiers (B1-B5) work equally well and there is no reason to choose one system over another.

Buffers

Other, than the three components (HCR Probes, Amplifier and Buffers), of an HCR RNA-FISH bundle, there are additional ancillary buffers you will need to prepare to conduct the experiment. The buffers (e.g., 5 x SSCT, 4% PFA, etc.) needed are detailed in our protocols that can be found on our homepage: HCR Protocols.
